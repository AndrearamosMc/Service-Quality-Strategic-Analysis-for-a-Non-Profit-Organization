# Service-Quality-Strategic-Analysis-for-a-Non-Profit-Organization

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the quality of service provided by promoters of a national non-profit organization in Mexico, with the goal of identifying operational gaps and proposing data-driven strategies to improve service performance, satisfaction, and social impact.

The project follows a consulting-style approach, combining survey analysis, dashboards, statistical testing, and strategic recommendations based on real organizational data.

---

##  Objectives
- Measure perceived service quality using the SERVQUAL model  
- Identify strengths and improvement areas across regions, roles, and institutional segments  
- Analyze internal processes related to collaboration and fundraising  
- Generate actionable insights to support strategic decision-making  

---

##  Methodology
- Quantitative and descriptive analysis  
- Survey design based on SERVQUAL dimensions:
  - Tangibles
  - Reliability
  - Responsiveness
  - Empathy
  - Overall service quality, information, and recommendation
- Data segmentation by state, role, institutional type, and relationship duration  
- Statistical validation using ANOVA  
- Process mapping for operational analysis  

---

## Analysis & Tools
- Survey data analysis and KPI definition  
- Interactive dashboards for service quality indicators  
- Geographic and role-based performance comparisons  
- Statistical analysis using ANOVA  
- Process diagrams to identify inefficiencies and improvement opportunities  

---

##  Key Insights
- Service quality perception is consistent across several dimensions, with empathy and tangible elements identified as key improvement areas  
- Performance varies by region and institutional segment, supporting the need for targeted strategies  
- Promoter engagement and response time directly impact satisfaction and recommendation  
- Internal organizational well-being is closely linked to external service quality
